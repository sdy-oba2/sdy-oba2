Timer

A timer shows the progression of time in the system. The timer can be used to show elapsed time of a certain process, or the remaining time for a set function. The frame below shows the general image of our defined timer component.

In addition to the general anatomy of the defined timer, below, we also provide designers and engineers with a closer look at the Time Display component's structure.

For the Time Display component, our design system employed the number-1 token (font-secondary-700(Roboto), font-size-1100(45px), line-height-900(53px)) and for the Sub-Text component, our design system employed the font-primary-400, font-size-50, and line-height-50 tokens respectively for the text. The Time Display component employs the token XXX-Content-Palette.Primary (with XXX correlated to the theme in use) for its text color. On the other hand, the Sub-Text component employs the token XXX-Content-Palette.Secondary (with XXX correlated to the theme in use) for its text color.

Structurally, the Time Display component is always positioned at the center of the Timer component, and the Sub-Text component is positioned directly below it. There should be a minimum distance of spacing-1200 from the Time Display component to the Track to maintain the Timer component's visibility. Similar to the anatomy section, in this section, we also provide designers with a closer look at the Time Display component's behavior in an established environment.

Time Display Mask

To ensure the animation is visible to users while maintaining the function's integrity, a mask with the same width and a height of line-height-900 (53px) as the Time Display component is applied directly on top of the component. This mask employs the clipping property, allowing numbers to be clipped off once they go pass the border of the mask. The illustration below shows the position and the size of this mask in relative to the Time Display component.

Animation - Easing

To ensure a smooth animation that enhance the user's experience and the application's aesthetic, our design system employs the easing-slow (cubic-bezier(.25,.5,.5,.1)) token for the animation's easing and a duration-quick (0.25s or 8 frames with 30fps standard) token for the Minutes Display and Seconds Display's animations. On the other hand, for the Separator component, our design system employs the easing-none (no easing) with a transition duration of duration-prompt (0.05s or 2 frames with 30fps standard) and an intermission duration of duration-slow (0.4s or 12 frames with 30fps standard).
