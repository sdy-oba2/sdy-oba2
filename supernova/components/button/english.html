Button

Buttons allow users to perform an action or mark a selection. They’re used for similar, task-based options within a workflow, and are ideal for interfaces where buttons aren’t meant to draw a lot of attention. For the One Button Application V2, only two types of Button will be provided.

  • Filled Button
  • Outlined Button

Filled Button

Used as primary action button to emphasize a certain actionable entry

Outlined Button

Used as secondary action button

Designers can clone our defined buttons from below.

Construction

Our provided button components have a minimum height and width of scaling-1200 to ensure the most optimal interactive experience for users. Furthermore, to ensure visual harmony with other components, our button components are designed with a margin of spacing-50. For a disinctive look, our button components are also designed with a border-radius-large. The details of these tokens can be found in their respective pages in the Foundation section of our guidelines. The button components provided by our design system employs the token subtitle for the text in Button Label.

Avoid Icons & Emoji

Both designers and engineers should avoid using icons and emoji with the provided button components. Only use descriptive and concise text to avoid confusing users about the purpose of the designed button.

Avoid Overiding Colors

Both designers and engineers should adhere to using only the specified color tokens for the provided buttons. Do not overide the colors of the buttons to avoid affecting the brand's image negatively and to avoid causing confusion among users.

Maintain Visibility

When using the provided button components, designers and engineers must avoid containers and backgrounds with colors that may affect the button's visibility. All of the design created following our design guidelines and documents must maintain compliance with the WCAG 2.1 standard.

Button Groups

A combination of multiple buttons from the provided button components to create button groups is recommended when the needs to show additional actions in related to the primary action is presented. In such case, the provided Filled Button should be prioritized for actions of higher importance or priority, while the Outlined Button can be used for any actions that are not important or not recommended.

Be Concise and Descriptive

The button's label must be written concisely with a recommended length of 1-2 words and a maximum length of 4 words. The label should be descriptive and short to give the users a quick understanding what function or action may entail when the button is triggered. It is recommended to write the label in verb form to reflect the action or function that the user is undertaking.

Be Aware of the Tone

Emoji, exclamation marks, and puns are inappropriate for the nature of the utilitarian buttons. Keep the button label to simple text with no extra decoration and punctuation.
